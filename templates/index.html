Features & Tools

Tracking Stars

This robot spins at approximately the same rate as the Earth’s rotation so that the camera angle stays the same when a camera/phone is taking a long exposure shot of a deep sky object.

Detecting Stars

From the taken picture of robots, the number of stars can be detected by the program using OpenCV.

Hardware

For the robot, we used Step motor with reduction gear, Cogwheel, Arduino, Tripod, 3d-printed body, and Motor driver.

Software

For the robot and this website, we used C++, Python, HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and Flask.

Demo

The main parts of Star Tracking Robot

A. Body

The star tracker is made up of a step motor with reduction gear, Cogwheel, Arduino, a tripod, a 3d-printed body, a motor driver, and a laser pointer.

B. Arduino Uno Board

The Arduino Uno Board controls the motor driver so that the motor spins the small, metal cogwheel at a constant rate of 1/48°/second. The large and small cogwheels have a ratio of 5:1, making the large cogwheel spin at a rate of 1/240°/second, which is the rate of the earth’s rotation.
